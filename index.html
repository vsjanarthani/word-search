Neanderthal

en-us

verb

An extint species of human that was widely distributed in ice-age Europe between c. 120,00-35,000 years ago, with a receding forehead and prominent brow ridges. The Neanderthals were associated with the Mousterian flint industry of the Middle Paleolithic

Meaning: an uncivilized, unintelligent, or uncouth person (typically used of a man)

Short Definitions: extinct hominid

Example: the stereotype of the mechanic as a macho Neanderthal

Synonyms: 1. One
